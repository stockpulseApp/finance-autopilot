## 1. X Thread (10 tweets max)

1/ Your wealth routine doesn't need hours—it needs 15 minutes of *intentional* focus. Here's the framework we use to help people stop reacting emotionally and start building systematically. 🧵

2/ Step 1: Define the outcome. What's your actual target? A specific dollar amount? Debt reduction? Risk mitigation? Without clarity, you'll chase shiny products instead of building a repeatable system.

3/ Step 2: Quantify your baseline. List every account, debt, and obligation. Cut one redundant fee today. This takes 20 minutes and often reveals $100-500/year in quick wins.

4/ Step 3: Pick ONE system. One app. One account structure. One habit. Most people fail because they're juggling 5 tools instead of mastering 1.

5/ Step 4: Automate execution. Remove willpower from the equation. Set automatic transfers, contributions, and reviews. Let compounding work while you sleep.

6/ Step 5: Review quarterly—not daily. Checking balances obsessively leads to overcorrection. Change course only on *data*, not headlines or fear.

7/ The biggest mistake? Chasing the "best" product before stabilizing cash flow. Build process first. Optimize tools second.

8/ Your 15-minute weekly money review beats sporadic 2-hour deep dives every time. Consistency > intensity. System > willpower.

9/ Try this: Week 1 = audit. Week 2 = implement. Week 3 = stress-test (what if you lost your job?). Week 4 = optimize.

10/ Ready? Start with one account audit today. One fee cut. One weekly 20-minute review on your calendar. That's the foundation. Everything else compounds from there.
