## 1) X Thread (10 tweets max)

1/ Building wealth doesn't require hours of grinding. It requires 15 minutes of *intentional* routine. Here's the framework that actually works:

2/ Step 1: Define the outcome. What's your target? Pay off debt? Hit $X saved? Risk reduction? Be specific. Vague goals = vague results.

3/ Step 2: Quantify your baseline. List every account, every obligation, every dollar flowing in/out. You can't optimize what you don't measure.

4/ Step 3: Pick ONE system. One app. One account structure. One habit. Most people fail by trying to do everything at once.

5/ Step 4: Automate execution. Remove willpower from the equation. If it requires daily decisions, it will fail. Set it and forget it.

6/ Step 5: Review quarterly. Let data, not headlines or emotions, drive changes. One 20-minute weekly check-in. That's it.

7/ Common mistake: Chasing the "perfect" product instead of building a repeatable process. The best system is the one you'll actually use.

8/ Wealth compounds on consistency, not intensity. Build the routine. Automate it. Let time do the work. 15 minutes changes everything.
