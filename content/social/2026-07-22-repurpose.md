## 🧵 X Thread (10 tweets max)

1/ Most investors understand dollar-cost averaging. The problem? They stop doing it when the market feels scary—exactly when it matters most.

You freeze. You pause contributions. You wait for "things to calm down."

Then you miss the gains. Here's why DCA actually works:

2/ DCA = investing the same $ amount at regular intervals, regardless of price.

$500/month buys MORE shares when prices drop, FEWER when they spike.

The math is mechanical. You don't need to predict anything.

3/ Maya paused her $500/month Roth IRA contributions in mid-2024 after reading about "bubble valuations." 18 months later, she's still waiting for the "safe" entry point.

She missed $9,000 in contributions + the gains on those dollars.

The market never felt safe enough.

4/ Compare that to James: $400/month to his 401(k), same schedule through volatility spikes, rate hikes, geopolitical drama, crashes—everything.

His October contribution bought 8.7% MORE shares when markets dropped 8%. When prices recovered, he owned more of the gain.

5/ The real cost of timing the market: research shows retail investors underperform by 3-6% annually trying to call tops and bottoms.

DCA doesn't require being smarter than the market. Just more disciplined than your emotions.

6/ Someone who contributed $500/month from Jan 2018–Dec 2025 invested $48,000 through record highs, COVID crashes, 2022 bear market, and geopolitical jitters.

Account value: ~$71,000 (47.9% gain).

Market timers? Usually way behind.

7/ The S&P 500 hit all-time highs in Jan 2018. Investors who stopped contributing then "because valuations looked stretched" missed:
- 31.5% gains in 2019
- The entire recovery from 2020-2025

All because they thought expensive = never going higher.

8/ Automation is the secret weapon. Melissa forgot contributions during busy months. David set up auto-transfers on the 3rd of every month—never missed once in 3 years.

He doesn't even know what the market did last month.

Willpower is overrated.

9/ How to actually do this:
- Set sustainable contribution amount (not tied to market conditions)
- Auto-invest on the same day each month
- Choose a diversified, low-cost fund
- Turn off price alerts
- Never pause for market timing

That's it.

10/ When markets feel scary, remember: that's exactly when DCA delivers its mathematical advantage. You're buying more shares when prices drop, fewer when they spike.

The expensive shares today are only expensive if prices never go higher.

History suggests otherwise. 📈
