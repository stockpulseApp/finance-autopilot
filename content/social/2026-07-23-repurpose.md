# Repurposed Content

## 1) X Thread (10 Tweets Max)

1/ Self-custody = complete control + complete responsibility. The moment you move crypto off an exchange, there's no customer support team to reverse mistakes. No password reset email. Everything is irreversible. Here's how to do it right. 🧵

2/ **Why it matters:** Exchanges are IOUs. If they're hacked, go bankrupt, or freeze your account, your access vanishes. Self-custody eliminates counterparty risk—but introduces operational risk. You become the single point of failure.

3/ The foundation: a private key. It's a long random number that proves ownership and signs transactions. Lose it, lose your crypto forever. Share it, lose your crypto immediately. This is why setup matters more than anything you do after.

4/ Your wallet gives you a seed phrase (12-24 words) that regenerates your private key. Write it on paper. Never digitally. Store it in a fireproof safe. This phrase is your last resort if your wallet is lost, stolen, or broken.

5/ **Hot wallets** (software): Convenient for daily use + dApp access. Private key lives on your device. Best for amounts you're willing to lose if compromised. Examples: Coinbase Wallet, Trust Wallet, Rabby Wallet.

6/ **Cold wallets** (hardware): Maximum security. Private key never touches the internet. Ideal for long-term holdings. Cost $60–$150. One theft, hack, or device failure and you're covered—just restore from your seed phrase on a new device.

7/ **Multi-signature wallets**: Require multiple keys to move funds. A 2-of-3 setup means two of three keys must sign. Eliminates single-point-of-failure risk. Essential for institutions. Worth it for individuals holding $100k+.

8/ **Setup checklist:** Choose your wallet → Generate seed phrase → Write it down on paper → Store in secure location → Test recovery on a second device → Never screenshot, email, or digitally store the phrase.

9/ Mistakes that cost thousands: reusing addresses for privacy, falling for phishing sites, trusting unofficial wallet downloads, not testing recovery before moving large sums, storing seed phrases digitally.

10/ Self-custody makes sense if you hold $5k+ long-term, value control over convenience, and are willing to learn unfamiliar workflows. Otherwise, stick with an exchange. Most experienced users layer both: exchange for buying, hardware wallet for saving.
