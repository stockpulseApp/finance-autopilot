# Tax-Advantaged Accounts Guide

**Preview — full guide includes flowcharts and bracket-specific examples.**

Tax-advantaged accounts are the government’s clearest “coupon” for building wealth. This guide orders **where to contribute first** — the question most articles answer vaguely.

> Not tax advice. Rules change; confirm with a CPA for your situation.

---

## Preview: The usual priority stack (W-2 earner)

1. **401(k) to employer match** — instant return on contributions  
2. **HSA** (if on qualifying HDHP) — triple tax advantage for medical savers  
3. **Roth or Traditional IRA** — income limits apply  
4. **Max 401(k)** or additional employer plan space  
5. **Taxable brokerage** — after tax-advantaged buckets sized for goals  

Self-employed readers get a separate branch in the paid guide (SEP-IRA, Solo 401(k)).

---

## Preview: Roth vs Traditional (intuition)

| Choose Roth when… | Choose Traditional when… |
|------------------|---------------------------|
| Lower tax bracket today than retirement | High bracket now, expect lower later |
| Want tax-free withdrawals flexibility | Need current-year deduction |
| Early-career earnings growth ahead | Peak earning years |
