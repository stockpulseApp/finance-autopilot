## Preview: The three-fund idea (simplified)

| Sleeve | Role | Typical vehicle |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| US stocks | Core growth | Total US stock market index |
| International | Global diversification | Total international index |
| Bonds | Stability near goals | US aggregate bond index |

**Example allocation (illustrative only):**

- Age 25, long horizon: 70% stocks (55 US / 15 intl) · 30% bonds  
- Age 40: 60/40 stock-bond split within above framework  
- Age 55 approaching spend: raise bonds; do not chase yield with junk credit  

Exact tickers depend on your broker’s fund family (Vanguard, Fidelity, Schwab equivalents exist).

## Preview: Broker selection criteria

Before opening an account, score brokers on:

1. **Expense ratios** on index funds (under 0.10% for broad funds is common)  
2. **Fractional shares** for small monthly buys  
3. **Tax lots** — specific identification vs. FIFO for taxable accounts  
4. **Automatic investing** — set and forget  
5. **IRA vs. taxable** fee differences  

**Full guide includes:** side-by-side table of major brokers, fund ticker mapping, and “if you only open one account” decision tree.
