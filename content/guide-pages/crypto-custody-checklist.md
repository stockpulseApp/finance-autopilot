Crypto on an exchange is an **IOU**. Self-custody means you hold the keys — and **you** are the bank. Mistakes are irreversible. This checklist is for people who already understand volatility risk and want operational security, not price predictions.

> Most long-term wealth builders treat crypto as a **small satellite** position after emergency fund and diversified index investing. Size accordingly.

---

## Custody models (pick consciously)

| Model | You hold keys? | Trade-off |
|-------|----------------|-----------|
| Exchange (CEX) | No | Convenient; counterparty + hack risk |
| Self-custody hot wallet | Yes (software) | Control; malware/phishing risk |
| Hardware wallet | Yes (offline device) | Best for meaningful holdings |
| Paper / steel backup | Backup only | Fire/water plan required |

**Rule:** Amount you cannot afford to lose in a scam → hardware wallet + tested backup. Pocket money can stay on exchange if you accept risk.

---

## Setup checklist (new wallet)

- [ ] Buy hardware wallet **only from manufacturer website** — never eBay/Amazon third-party  
- [ ] Initialize device yourself; device should generate seed **on device**  
- [ ] Write seed on paper/steel — **never** photo, cloud, email, Notes app  
- [ ] Verify receive address on **hardware screen** before sharing  
- [ ] Send **small test transaction** before large transfer  
- [ ] Document wallet software version and supported chains  

---

## Seed phrase rules

Your 12/24 words = full access. Anyone with words steals everything.

**Do:**
- Split backup locations (home safe + bank box) if large holdings  
- Consider passphrase (25th word) if wallet supports BIP39 passphrase  
- Practice **restore on spare device** once with small amount  

**Never:**
- Type seed into any website, “support” chat, or Google form  
- Enter seed into phone camera “recovery” apps  
- Share screen during “help” video calls  

Legitimate support **never** asks for your seed.

## Scam patterns (2024–2026)

1. **Fake support** — DMs on X/Telegram/Discord offering “refund” or “sync wallet”  
2. **Address poisoning** — attacker sends dust from address visually similar to yours; you copy wrong address from history  
3. **Malicious approvals** — signing unlimited token approvals on sketchy DeFi sites  
4. **Airdrop / claim sites** — connect wallet = drain  
5. **Romance / investment groups** — guaranteed returns; withdrawal fees to “release” funds  

**Defense:** Bookmark official sites. Revoke token approvals periodically with reputable tools. Whitelist addresses in your wallet app.

## Exchange hygiene (if you still use CEX)

- Unique strong password + password manager  
- Hardware 2FA (YubiKey) not SMS when available  
- Withdrawal allowlist + delay feature  
- No reusing email from data breaches  
- Understand proof-of-reserves is incomplete assurance

## Tax and compliance (US-oriented overview)

The IRS treats many digital assets as property. Sales and trades can be taxable events. Keep records: date, USD value, fees. Use export CSV from exchanges. Consult a CPA familiar with crypto — especially for staking, mining, and cross-chain bridges.

This guide does not provide tax advice.

## Incident response

**If seed may be compromised:**  
Move assets immediately to **new** wallet with **new** seed on clean computer. Do not reuse old seed.

**If only exchange compromised:**  
Change passwords, revoke API keys, contact exchange fraud line, file reports as advised.

**If scammed:**  
Document txs on block explorer; report to FBI IC3; accept recovery is rare — focus on preventing repeat.

## When self-custody is overkill

Small balances, frequent trading, or learning with $50 — regulated exchange with 2FA may be rational. Re-evaluate as balance grows.
