## What is an index fund?

An index fund owns hundreds or thousands of stocks (or bonds) to match a market index like the S&P 500. You get **instant diversification** without picking individual companies.

## Why beginners start here

- **Low fees** — Expense ratios often under 0.10%.
- **Market returns** — Historically, most active managers underperform after fees.
- **Less behavior risk** — Fewer decisions means fewer chances to panic-sell.

## Index fund vs ETF

Both can track the same index. **Mutual funds** may auto-invest exact dollar amounts; **ETFs** trade like stocks during market hours. For buy-and-hold investors, either works — pick the one your brokerage makes easiest.

## A starter portfolio (example)

- US total stock market index — 70%
- International stock index — 20%
- US bond index — 10%

Adjust bond percentage based on age and risk tolerance.

## How to buy your first fund

1. Open a brokerage or retirement account (IRA/401k).
2. Search for a low-cost total market or S&P 500 index fund.
3. Set up automatic monthly investments.
4. Ignore daily headlines.

## Bottom line

Index funds are the default tool for building wealth over decades. Keep costs low, invest consistently, and let compounding work.
